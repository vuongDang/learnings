# AI Governance

- Hard law: punishment when not respected
- Soft law: voluntary but some mechanims give it real impact
  - easier to pass them
  - market pressure, maybe the clients will ask for compliance with it
  - reputational sales, civil society pressure you to comply
  - regulatory anticipation: competitive edge when it's harden later
  - liability in court: voluntary standards = evidence of "reasonable practice"

## Ex-ante / ex-post

- Ex-ante: control before the product is deployed
- Ex-post: control when there are consequences

## Enforcement mechanisms

- financial penalties
- criminal liability
- market exclusion 
- reputational pressure 
- procurement requirements 
- 

## Who regulates 

- legislature: eu parliament, us congress - very slow
- regulatory agency: FTC, EU AI Office, CAC - moderate
- industry / self-reg: ai labs, consortia - very fast
  
## Principles vs rules
  
- principles: broad outcomes to achieve ("be safe")
  - hard to punish/defend since it's not precise
- rules: precise, enumerable requirements
  - easier to audit ex-ante 
  - rules can be gamed and miss the goal
- neither is superior and both are necessary

## Atlas: Governance - Systemic Challenges

__Issue race dynamics__
- AI safety is a prisonner problem 
  - everyone benefits from collaborating on AI safety
  - those who move fastest gain competitive advantage
- Companies race to gain economic advantage and are pressured by ivestors 
  - openAI started as a safety-focues small nonprofit
  - Anthropic updated its RSP towards less ai safety
  - every new model release or technical advancements 
  is answered by rivals with something similar
  - the motto: "move fast and break things"
    - models are released with known vulnerabilities
- Countries are racing to gain AI leadership
  - Putin (2017): "whoever becomes the leader in AI will become the ruler of the world"
  - countries fight for geopolitical dominance 
    - inducing both economy and military superiority
  - compared to companies racing it's a long term strategic positioning with a lot of hidden cards.
    - this makes the situation even more dramatic and urgent: you're racing againt potential capabilities of adversaries

__Potential solutions__
- reciprocal snap-back limites 
  - states publish caps on 
    - scale, weapon deployment, data center compute 
  - force competitors to similar commitment
- force audits with mandatory safety labels
  - third party safety audits
- containment 
  - control on usage 
  - advanced chips, real-time monitoring of the API 
  - customer background check
  - this slow the spread of dange
- secret-safe verification 
  - secure-enclaves, logs, zero-knwoledge proofs 
  - let auditors check safety without 
  firms having to reveal their architecture or weights
