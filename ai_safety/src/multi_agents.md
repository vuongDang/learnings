# Multi-agent 

## Specialists 
### 1. Control & Monitoring specialists

* **Alan Cooney (UK AI Safety Institute):**
* **Focus:** Empirical AI Control, Monitorability, and Red Teaming. He leads the "Autonomous Systems" workstream at the UK AISI.
* **Why contact:** He is a current mentor for the **MATS program** (Deadline: Jan 18). His stream specifically works on "Chain-of-Thought Monitorability" and using agents to catch data poisoning.
* **Affiliation:** UK AISI / MATS.


* **Buck Shlegeris (Redwood Research):**
* **Focus:** Applied AI Control. His team builds "Control" frameworks where untrusted models are wrapped in monitoring layers.
* **Why contact:** Redwood is one of the few places doing "industrial-grade" control work (preventing a model from taking over the GPU cluster it runs on).
* **Affiliation:** Redwood Research (Berkeley).



### 2. The "Debate & Scalable Oversight" Theorists

*These researchers focus on the "Prover vs. Verifier" games—making AI honest through competition.*

* **Geoffrey Irving (Google DeepMind):**
* **Focus:** The "Godfather" of AI Debate. He wrote the original *AI Safety via Debate* paper. His work now focuses on "Sparse Debate" (making agents debate efficiently).
* **Why contact:** For deep theoretical questions about whether debate actually works mathematically.
* **Affiliation:** Google DeepMind (Scalable Alignment Team).


* **Julian Michael (NYU / Meta):**
* **Focus:** NLP Alignment and Debate. He runs experiments testing if humans can actually judge debates between AI agents.
* **Why contact:** He bridges the gap between abstract theory and real Language Model experiments.
* **Affiliation:** NYU Center for Data Science.



### 3. The "Cooperative AI" Academics

*These researchers focus on "Social Dilemmas"—how to stop agents from fighting or colluding against humans.*

* **Prof. Jakob Foerster (Oxford University):**
* **Focus:** Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL). He runs the **FLAIR Lab** at Oxford. His work on "Zero-Shot Coordination" is critical for ensuring agents don't misunderstand human intent.
* **Why contact:** If you are interested in the *math* of multi-agent cooperation (Game Theory).
* **Affiliation:** Oxford University.


* **Prof. David Krueger (Mila / Cambridge):**
* **Focus:** AI Safety & Multi-Agent Dynamics. He often speaks about "Goodhart's Law" (when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure) in multi-agent settings.
* **Why contact:** He is very active in the "AI Risk" community and often hires for "Governance" and "Control" technical roles.
* **Affiliation:** Mila (Quebec AI Institute).
