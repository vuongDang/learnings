# zkML 

Usage of Zero-Knowledge Cryptography for ai safety.

## Core goals 

- Runtime verification for inference 
- Audit verification

## Runtime verification

### Goals
- __verifiable inference__, a proof that the output was generated by a specific model with specific output 
- __model integrity & equality__, the model used for generated match the proof 
- __intellectual property__, the regulators (verifiers) do not learn anything about the model weights and architecture

### Tools
- EZKL 
- RISC Zero 

### Principle
1. Commitment scheme to publish a commitment/hash of their model weights
2. When the model processes an input the prover also generates a proof alongside the output
3. a verifier (regulator or smart contract) can check this proof in milliseconds

### Limits and challenges 
- __computational overhead__ proof generation is extremely resource-intensive. usually 1000x to 100000x times the resource 
required to produce the proven inference
- __fixed-point arithmetic__ modern ai use floating point numbers but ZK circuits require integers.
  The conversion introduces precision loss and potential vulnerabilities
- __scalability gap__ most practical zkML systems only handle up to millions of parameters whereas frontier models have trillions

### Technical breakdown 

- Build an arithmetic circuit which turn a model architecture (layers, neuron connection, activation function) into polynomial constraints
  - if you change the architecture you must write a new circuit
- Weights are usually not hardcoded into the circuit but are often used by the circuits as inputs everytime a proof is generated
  - weights are private witness 
  - you can change the weights without changing the circuit
  - weights can be encoded into the circuit 
    - pros: reduce number of constraints, faster proofs, implicit weight integrity 
    - cons: zero privacy (the circuit is public), retraining a model signifies recreating a circuit
- To make sure that the weights are valid and not changed maliciously we use commitment check (since they are private)
  - arithmetic circuit usually encompass two proofs
    - _ai_ciruit(input, weights) = output_
    - _AND Hash(Weights) == public_commitment_C_
  - hence the circuit enforce both a model architecture and the weights


## Audit verification 

AI providers must prove that their models meet regulations like the EU AI Act
- Using protocols like FairZK a company can prove that their model do not rely on protected attributes (e.g. race or gender)
- it can also prove that a model does not contain knowledge about specific banned data
- provides regulators mathematical proof of compliance rather than reports

- __Regulatory Compliance__ demonstrate that a model adheres to safety standards without revealing sensitive data
